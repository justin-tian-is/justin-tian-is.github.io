About Me

Welcome to my academic portfolio. I am a PhD candidate in Information Systems at the University of Florida's Warrington College of Business, working under the supervision of Dr. Liangfei Qiu. My research focuses on the societal impact of emerging technologies, particularly exploring how innovations like Generative AI, autonomous vehicles, and digital health platforms reshape human behavior and social outcomes.

I am currently on the academic job market, seeking faculty positions where I can contribute to cutting-edge research at the intersection of technology and society. My work employs rigorous econometric methods, machine learning, and field experiments to understand the complex relationships between technological adoption and societal well-being.

Research Interests

Societal Impact of Emerging Technologies

My primary research stream investigates how cutting-edge technologies‚Äîincluding Generative AI, autonomous vehicles (robotaxis), and digital platforms‚Äîinfluence human behavior, social equity, and economic outcomes. I examine both intended and unintended consequences of technological adoption, with particular attention to issues of digital divide and accessibility.

Health Information Technology

I study the transformative effects of digital health innovations on healthcare delivery and patient outcomes. My work explores telemedicine adoption patterns, digital mental health services, and the role of physician peer networks in online healthcare platforms. This research has significant implications for healthcare policy and practice.

Economics of Technology Platforms

My research examines economic dynamics within digital platforms, including e-commerce live streaming, ride-hailing services, and online gambling platforms. I investigate how platform design choices affect user behavior, market outcomes, and societal welfare, employing both experimental and observational methods.

Methodological Expertise
