About Me

Welcome to my academic portfolio. I am a PhD candidate in Information Systems at the University of Florida's Warrington College of Business, working under the supervision of Dr. Liangfei Qiu. My research focuses on the societal impact of technologies, particularly exploring how innovations like Generative AI, AI, autonomous vehicles, health IT, and other emerging digital technologies reshape human behavior and social outcomes.

Research

Research Interests

Societal Impact of Technologies

My primary research stream investigates how cutting-edge technologies—including Generative AI, AI, autonomous vehicles (robotaxis), and digital platforms—influence human behavior, social welfare, and economic outcomes. I examine both intended and unintended consequences of technological adoption, with particular attention to issues of digital divide and inequality.

Health Information Technology

I study the transformative effects of digital health innovations on healthcare delivery and patient outcomes. My work explores telemedicine adoption patterns, digital mental health services, and the role of physician peer networks in online healthcare platforms. This research has significant implications for healthcare policy and practice.

Economics of Emerging Technologies

My research examines economic dynamics within digital platforms, including e-commerce live streaming, ride-hailing services, and online gambling platforms. I investigate how platform design choices affect user behavior, market outcomes, and societal welfare, employing both experimental and observational methods.

Methodological Expertise

  • Econometrics: Advanced causal inference methods, natural experiments, difference-in-differences
  • Machine Learning: Predictive modeling, data analytics, deep learning
  • Field Experiments: Large-scale randomized controlled trials in digital environments
